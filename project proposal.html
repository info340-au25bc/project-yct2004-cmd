Cybersecurity Education Platform: Democratizing Security Education

Problem/Data Domain

Cybersecurity awareness represents a critical challenge in our increasingly digital world, where individuals and organizations face constant threats from phishing attacks, malware, social engineering, and data breaches. Despite the growing sophistication of cyber threats, many people lack fundamental knowledge about online safety practices, password security, and recognizing common attack vectors. According to the 2023 Verizon Data Breach Investigations Report, 74% of breaches involve human error, highlighting a significant gap in cybersecurity literacy across all demographics. This knowledge deficit affects everyone from students and remote workers to small business owners, making cybersecurity education not just a technical issue but a social imperative.

The information technology problem in this space is the lack of accessible, engaging, and personalized tools for both delivering and assessing cybersecurity knowledge. While corporate training programs exist, they're often expensive and inaccessible to individual learners. Educational resources are frequently static and don't provide interactive ways to test comprehension or track improvement over time. Existing solutions like KnowBe4 and SANS Security Awareness focus primarily on enterprise clients with subscription models, while free resources like Google's Phishing Quiz or the National Cyber Security Centre's online materials offer limited interactivity and no community-driven content creation. App Description

This application will help people who are unable to access informational spaces regarding cybersecurity terminology and concepts. More specifically, it aims to help students study for certifications such as the CompTIA Security+ while also forming groups that can lead to lasting connections within the cybersecurity community, providing opportunities for mentorship and career growth.

In this app, people will be able to:

  • Get directed to free relevant cybersecurity resources
  • Create and join study groups
  • Collaborate by making questions related to the sources they reviewed
  • Get ranked and compete with others through a point system
  • Give feedback in the event of a mistake by the question creator

The resources recommended by the app will include videos, articles, and textbooks curated by experts in the field. Examples include the Professor Messer Security+ Course, a highly rated collection of free videos that cover all the topics needed to pass the Security+ exam, and the CompTIA Security+ Study Guide, a comprehensive textbook covering the same material.

The app aims to:

  • Help people new to cybersecurity access free resources to learn the basics
  • Create a community of learners who support each other's growth
  • Make learning about cybersecurity fun and engaging through competition
